What is Portfolio?

A portfolio showcases your talents and skills. Typically, a portfolio includes items such as certificates, transcripts, samples of past work.

As the name suggests, portfolio is more detailed and descriptive document.

What is a career portfolio?

Finding the answer to “What is a career portfolio?” can help you supplement your resume with evidence of your work and skills.

A career portfolio is a collection of examples and materials that highlight the professional background of candidates. A professional portfolio may include work samples, resume, certifications and other materials that show your knowledge and achievements. Candidates can present their career portfolios to employers during the application or interview process. A professional career portfolio can reflect the work experience and qualifications of candidates. Many professionals personalise their portfolios and add specific work examples. For example, a content writer can add sample articles and blogs to their career portfolio. There are many job roles that may require candidates to have a career portfolio. Some common examples of such job roles can be instructors, makeup artists, photographers, designers, hair stylists and cake decorators.

ATS

What is ATS?

Applicant Tracking System

An ATS is a computer software program that manages the hiring process. It does this by collecting and sorting thousands of resumes.

Hiring managers can then screen candidates using the ATS, as well as track their progress through the hiring process. By digitizing the hiring process in this way, an ATS saves employers time and money.

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to manage the application process. This is a system that collects, scans and ranks your resume.

What is ATS friendly resume?

An ATS compliant resume is one that can be easily scanned and interpreted by an applicant tracking system (ATS).
